It boasts an impressive nutritional profile, offering an abundance of essential vitamins, minerals, and bioactive compounds that contribute to overall well-being. Curd is a rich source of calcium, which plays a crucial role in maintaining strong bones, teeth, and nails. It also contains phosphorus, which works synergistically with calcium to support bone health and energy metabolism. Furthermore, curd is a good source of protein, providing essential amino acids required for building and repairing tissues, producing hormones and enzymes, and supporting immune function. Its protein content also contributes to satiety, promoting feelings of fullness and helping to manage body weight. Additionally, curd is a good source of B vitamins, such as riboflavin, vitamin B12, and folate, which play vital roles in energy production, cell growth, and nervous system function. It also contains vitamin A, which is important for vision, skin health, and immune function. Curd is a fermented food, and the fermentation process produces beneficial bacteria known as probiotics. These probiotics support gut health by promoting a balanced gut microbiome, aiding digestion, and enhancing nutrient absorption. Curd also contains conjugated linoleic acid (CLA), a fatty acid that has been associated with several health benefits, including reduced body fat, improved insulin sensitivity, and anticancer properties.
