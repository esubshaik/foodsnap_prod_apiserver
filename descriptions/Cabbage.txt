It is a nutrient-dense vegetable, providing an array of essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants with low calories. Cabbage is a good source of vitamin C, a powerful antioxidant that helps protect cells from damage and boosts the immune system. It also contains vitamin K, essential for blood clotting and bone health. Rich in fiber, cabbage promotes a feeling of fullness, aids digestion, and supports gut health. Folate, a B vitamin crucial for cell growth and DNA synthesis, is abundantly present in cabbage. It is also a good source of potassium, a mineral that helps regulate blood pressure and supports nerve and muscle function. Cabbage contains antioxidants, including flavonoids and carotenoids, which protect cells from oxidative stress, reducing the risk of chronic diseases. Its anti-inflammatory properties may benefit those with inflammatory conditions. Cabbage is versatile and can be incorporated into various dishes, from salads and stir-fries to soups and stews, making it an easy way to boost overall nutrient intake and support a healthy lifestyle.
