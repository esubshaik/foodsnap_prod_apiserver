It's a powerhouse of vitamin C, providing 112% of the Daily Value (DV) in just one medium lemon. This essential nutrient supports immune function, collagen production, and iron absorption. Lemon is also a good source of potassium, supplying 9% of the DV. Potassium helps maintain healthy blood pressure, supports nerve function, and aids in muscle contraction. Furthermore, it contains small amounts of other essential vitamins and minerals, including folate, thiamin, vitamin B6, niacin, magnesium, and phosphorus. The dietary fiber in lemon, though modest, contributes to digestive health and satiety. Additionally, lemon is a versatile fruit that can be enjoyed in various ways. Adding lemon juice to water or tea is a refreshing and hydrating option. Its zest can infuse dishes with a vibrant citrus flavor and aroma. Moreover, lemon is a popular ingredient in many cuisines, enhancing the taste of salads, marinades, desserts, and beverages.
