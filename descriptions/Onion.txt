It is a versatile vegetable that offers a rich blend of essential nutrients. With only 45 calories per 100 grams, it is a low-calorie food that packs a powerful nutritional punch. Onions are a good source of dietary fiber, providing about 2 grams per 100 grams, which aids digestion and promotes a feeling of fullness. They are also a rich source of vitamin C, an essential nutrient for immune function and collagen synthesis. One medium onion provides about 14% of the recommended daily intake of vitamin C. Additionally, onions are a good source of vitamin B6, which plays a vital role in metabolism and the formation of red blood cells. They also contain potassium, manganese, and phosphorus, essential minerals for maintaining electrolyte balance, bone health, and energy production. Furthermore, onions contain various antioxidants, including quercetin and sulfur compounds, which help protect cells from damage and may reduce the risk of chronic diseases. Onions are commonly used to add flavor and depth to various dishes, from soups and stews to salads and sandwiches. Their unique pungent flavor comes from sulfur compounds, which also contribute to their potential health benefits. Whether eaten raw, cooked, or processed into extracts, onions offer a multitude of essential nutrients and bioactive compounds that contribute to overall health and well-being.
