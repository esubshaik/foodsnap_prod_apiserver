It contains vitamin C, which boosts immunity and promotes healthy skin and eyes. With its high fiber content, it aids digestion, regulates blood sugar levels, and promotes satiety. Additionally, it is a good source of B vitamins, which are crucial for energy production, metabolism, and nervous system function. The presence of antioxidants, such as carotenoids and flavonoids, in potatoes may help protect against chronic diseases and reduce inflammation. Furthermore, it is low in fat, sodium, and cholesterol, making it a heart-friendly choice. Its versatile nature allows for various cooking methods, including baking, boiling, roasting, and frying, making it a popular and enjoyable addition to many meals.
